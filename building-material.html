Building Materials

Explore the essential materials used in construction.

Sand (Maurang)

Sand is a crucial, versatile material widely used in construction, serving as a binding agent, filler, and even a base material for various applications. It's a key ingredient in concrete, mortar, and plaster, and is also used for filling, leveling, and improving drainage in construction projects.

Bricks (Eta)

Bricks are a foundational building material, widely used in construction for their strength, durability, and versatility. They are primarily used to construct walls, pavements, and other structural elements. Bricks can be made from various materials, including clay, shale, or concrete, and are known for their fire resistance and ability to store heat, contributing to energy efficiency in buildings.

Grey River Sand (Balu)

Grey river sand, a naturally occurring sand sourced from riverbeds, is a commonly used material in construction due to its smooth, rounded particles and good workability. It's primarily used in concrete production, mortar mixing, and plastering.

Crushed Stone (Gitti)

Crushed stone, made by breaking down rock into smaller pieces, is a versatile construction aggregate with numerous uses. It's primarily used for road construction, as a base material for foundations and pathways, and in concrete and asphalt mixes. Its strength, durability, and drainage properties make it a suitable choice for various applications.
